Ca2CuTe4O10Cl2, a new synthetic tellurium(IV) oxochloride

R. Takagi and M. Johnsson

Synopsis: A new three-dimensional bimetallic tellurium halogenide, Ca2CuTe4O10Cl2, was synthesized by a transport reaction starting with CaO, CuO, CuCl2 and TeO2, and was characterized by single-crystal X-ray diffraction. The three-dimensional framework is built from anionic {[CuTe4O10Cl2]4}n- layers parallel to (00) and connected via Ca-O bonds. The anionic layers are formed by two types of polyhedra, namely centrosymmetric CuO4Cl2 octahedra and TeO4E distorted bipyramids (E being the 5s2 lone pair of TeIV).

Formula: Ca2CuTe4O10Cl2
